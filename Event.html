Japan Event Calendar
January February March April May June July August September October November December Reference
January

  • January 1 - 正月 New Year

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    This is the most important holiday in Japan. While only January 1 is designated as a national holiday, many businesses remain closed through January 3.

  • 2nd Monday of January - 成人の日 Coming of Age

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    The coming of age of 20 year old men and women is celebrated on this national holiday.

February

  • February 3 - 節分 Beginning of Spring

    #nationwide

    Setsubun is not a national holiday, but celebrated at shrines and temples nationwide.

  • February 11 - 建国記念の日 National Foundation Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    According to the earliest Japanese history records, on this day in the year 660 BC the first Japanese emperor was crowned.

  • February 14 - バレンタイン・デー Valentine's Day

    #nationwide

    In Japan, women give chocolates to men on Valentine's Day.

  • February 23 - 天皇の誕生日 Emperor's Birthday

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    The birthday of the current emperor is always a national holiday. If the emperor changes, the national holiday changes to the birthday date of the new emperor.

March

  • March 3 - 雛祭り Doll's Festival

    #nationwide

    On this day, families with girls wish their daughters a successful and happy life. Dolls are displayed in the house together with peach blossoms.

  • March 14 - ホワイトデー White Day

    #nationwide

    The opposite of Valentine's Day: Men give cakes or chocolates to women.

  • Around March 20/21 - 春分の日 Spring Equinox Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    Graves are visited during the week (春の彼岸 Haru no Higan) of the Equinox Day.

April

  • April 29 - 昭和の日 Showa Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    The birthday of former Emperor Showa. Before 2007, April 29 was known as Greenery Day (now celebrated on May 4). Showa Day is part of the Golden Week.

May

  • May 3 - 憲法記念日 Constitution Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    A national holiday remembering the new constitution, which was put into effect after the war.

  • May 4 - みどりの日 Greenery Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    Until 2006, Greenery Day was celebrated on April 29, the former Emperor Showa's birthday, due to the emperor's love for plants and nature. It is now celebrated on May 4 and is part of the Golden Week.

  • May 5 - こどもの日 Children's Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    Also called boy's festival.

June
July

  • Between July 7 and August 7 - たなばた Star Festival

    #nationwide

    Tanabata is a festival rather than a national holiday.

  • Around July 15 to August 15 - お盆 Obon Festival

    #nationwide

    Obon is a Buddhist event to commemorate deceased ancestors.

  • 3rd Monday of July - 海の日 Marine Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    A recently introduced national holiday to celebrate the ocean. The day marks the return of Emperor Meiji from a boat trip to Hokkaido in 1876.

August

  • Between July 7 and August 7 - たなばた Star Festival

    #nationwide

    Tanabata is a festival rather than a national holiday.

  • August 11 - 山の日 Mountain Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    Newly introduced in 2016, this national holiday celebrates mountains.

  • Around July 15 to August 15 - お盆 Obon Festival

    #nationwide

    Obon is a Buddhist event to commemorate deceased ancestors.

September

  • 3rd Monday of September - 敬老の日 Respect for the Aged Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    Respect for the elderly and longevity are celebrated on this national holiday.

  • Around September 22/23 - 秋分の日 Autumn Equinox Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    Graves are visited during the week of the Equinox Day.

October

  • 2nd Monday of October - 体育の日 Health and Sports Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    On that day in 1964, the Olympic games of Tokyo were opened.

November

  • November 3 - 文化の日 Culture Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    A day for promotion of culture and the love of freedom and peace. On culture day, schools and the government award selected persons for their special, cultural achievements. The day was originally a public holiday because it was Emperor Meiji's birthday. Later in 1946, the post-war constitution was promulgated on this date which led to the establishment of Culture Day.

  • November 15 - 七五三 Shichi-Go-San

    #nationwide

    Girls of age three and seven and boys of age three and five are celebrated on Shichigosan around November 15, and it is prayed for their good health and growth.

  • November 23 - 勤労感謝の日 Labor Thanksgiving Day

    #nationwide #national_holiday

    A national holiday for honoring labour.

December

  • December 24-25 - クリスマス Christmas

    #nationwide

    Shopping malls are heavily decorated in the weeks leading up to Christmas, and many people follow some local Christmas traditions, such as eating chicken and a Christmas cake or having a dinner with one's partner.

  • December 31 - 大晦日 Ōmisoka

    #nationwide

    A Japanese traditional celebration on the last day of the year. Traditionally, it was held on the final day of the 12th lunar month. With Japan's switch to using the Gregorian calendar at the beginning of the Meiji era, December 31 is now used for the celebration.
